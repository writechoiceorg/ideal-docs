What is idx? What does it do?
idx is a Government-to-Government (G2G) data exchange layer and API/Service Hub for agencies to consume governmental compliance, certifications, and data for their applications. It aims to consolidate and digitize all documents held by the Government so that it is the provider of information for citizens or entities.
What are the benefits of using idx for my government agency?
The benefits of using idx include improved efficiency, better integration with citizen-facing platforms, and cost savings. By reducing bureaucratic processes and providing immediate access to information, idx can help streamline workflows and speed up administrative processes. Additionally, integrating with idx enables agencies to capture compliance, certification, and additional data from other agencies, improving the quality of services provided to citizens.
How do I get started with consuming idx data?
To get started with consuming idx data, you will need to register your agency with idx, obtain credentials, and develop an integration plan. You can then test your integration in a staging or testing environment and coordinate with the idx support team to prepare your integration for deployment to the production environment.
What kind of data is available through idx?
IDX currently provides data from 11 agencies. The data available through idx includes compliance, certification, and additional data that can be used by agencies and systems to provide services to citizens.
